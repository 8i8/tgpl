… What on earth is that?

This is an important question to ask but not one that can be answered easily nor quickly, any simple explanation would be all to readily cast aside by the discerning mind as a belief or superstition. Yet this practise, its accompanying doctrine, are from their core foundation deeply scientific; Who then among us, that is truly of a scientific inclination, would not be intrigued to learn of any such thing as a science that bears the fruit of a predictive capacity, fully capable of withstanding the rigorous testing of the scientific method? Before any real understanding of this science might arise in the mind of the student, as is so for any other, one must begin by learning some of the language, the tools and techniques required to begin to explore and communicate the inherent depths of this wonderful subject.

Now it is also worthy of mention, I think that perhaps you will agree, that any scientific mind deserving of their accolade, should be quite aware today of the conundrum that is currently posed by the imperfections of the standard model; How the field of physics and its main stay tool mathematics, are today rather at a loss to describe their own innate limits, limits which are present in the very fruits of the practice; It is for this reason that I hope to spark some interest in the reader, that they consider this somewhat differing approach to scientific study and maths. To assist in the understanding of such as the oddities as the quantum state or the strangeness of the neuroepigenome that requires such divisive models as these; All of which seeming to never quite manage to fully describe the system. These terms and models, in time, have become accepted as fact by the more junior scholar and are thus often questioned no further, this is not so in the practice of jyotiṣa which always seeks to keep the students mind alert to the very limited nature of any teaching. A quintessential difference perhaps between the two schools, please do feel free to prove me wrong on this score; I long for only that.

In essence the main difference between jyotiṣa and modern physics is a differing models and different teaching methods, the foundations thus are so far removed the one from the other, that it is hard for any who are deeply schooled in one discipline, to understand the thought process required to grasp the other, often rejecting it outright as superstition all the while accepting premises of another doctrine as truth; Both however are in every way as difficult to grasp and to explain rationally. Take the widely used standard model of the atom for example, this notion requires that one collapse their own thought down into one dimension in order that it make any sense; Yet still we find ourselves imagining the world, built from these tiny one dimensional blocks; This notion itself is erroneous, we are in effect applying this one dimensional model to three spacial dimensions those that we perceive so easily with our eyes, rather than just the one dimension that is required to be justified by the limited mathematical tools. This is however, a very normal behaviour, one which I have no gripe with, I request only that you, dear reader, bear in mind this thought; The knowledge is still only a model, and has never been considered to be an established fact by those who understand and use it. That there might be some room left in your minds eye, to consider the possibilities and implications of another. A model that might also serves us well in our quest understand the marvels of the world in which we live; It is my contention that these two differing scientific approaches are much closer today than one might first think. 

              
                ज्योतिष | ज्योति-इष

jyoti → brightness, light

iṣa → measure, quantum

Jyotiṣa is the origin of the mathematical system that we use today introducing both the numerals and the concept of 0 as the decimal placeholder and the so called Arabic numerals originate from here by way of Arabian exploration of India in the distant past; This concept of 0 is itself a very intricate one, and śūnya is not ascribed lightly, paradoxically it is that light is also known by another term, śakti; However this little divergence is of a rather profound in nature, that I shall not attempt to explore with you dear reader, just yet. In essence jyotish uses a less rigid more colourful number system than that of western physics, this facet it would seem, is one of the major factors keeping the two disciplines form communicating with each other, or the practitioners of one from understanding the perspective of practitioners of the other.

So where to start? We are approaching a highly advanced subject, a science that can take many years, some say many lifetimes, to learn; To refine such a topic into a perspective for the beginner for lay person, the uninitiated. To be honest this is not at all an easy thing to do with out sounding, in English, like airy-fairy spiritual type or a religious fanatic, thought these terms are them selves born of another bread of fanaticism; I ask only that the reader keep an open mind throughout, try to assimilate the ideas presented as best they can, until such a time as, dear reader, you have a fare enough understanding of the subject at hand to really make up your own mind and not to propagate the thoughts of any other; I will make this promise to you now, the understanding gleaned from the study of this wonderful science, is well worth the mental effort required to assimilate its foible’s, the required thought patterns at first a challenge, once assimilated are a delight to explore. I shall try to keep my comparisons framed in a perspective that is understandable to one who has studied western decimal based mathematical science; The mathematical base of Jyotiṣa some what less linear, as is it usage. I also apologise to any who are spiritually inclined, though I think that your inclination will lead you to understand my predicament, in attempting to appeal to the broadest base of reader, that you will as such by the way of your own fair nature be able to excuse any apparent dryness in my words that this approach might instigate, please forgive my brashness in this circumstance; I hold nothing but admiration for your perspective.
